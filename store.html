What We Stock

Honest, evidence-based essentials — no hype. Here's what each one does, how to take it, and who it's for.

Pre-Workout

Energy & focus

A caffeine-based blend taken before training to boost energy, focus and output when you need it most.

Key benefits

  • Sharper focus and training drive
  • Caffeine (3–6 mg/kg) reliably lifts performance ~2–4%
  • Citrulline / beta-alanine may aid pumps & endurance
Dose1 scoop in 250–300 ml water (≈150–300 mg caffeine). Start with ½ scoop.
Timing20–40 min before your workout.
Best forEarly or low-energy sessions; heavy / high-intensity days.

Good to know: Avoid within ~6 hours of bedtime and don't stack with other caffeine. You don't need it every day — cycle it so it keeps working.

Creatine

Strength & power

The most researched supplement in sport — it helps your muscles produce quick energy for heavy, explosive efforts.

Key benefits

  • More strength, power and reps in the tank
  • Supports lean-muscle gains alongside training
  • Decades of evidence for safety & results
Dose3–5 g every day. Loading (20 g/day for 5–7 days) is optional.
TimingAny time — consistency beats timing. Post-workout with a meal works well.
Best forAnyone doing strength or high-intensity training.

Good to know: Myth-buster — creatine does not harm healthy kidneys and is not a steroid. A little early water weight in the muscle is normal. Plain monohydrate is the gold standard.

Whey Protein

Muscle & recovery

A fast-digesting, high-quality protein powder that makes hitting your daily protein target simple.

Key benefits

  • Repairs & builds muscle after training
  • ~20–25 g complete protein per convenient scoop
  • Helps satiety — useful on a fat-loss diet too
Dose1 scoop (~20–30 g). Aim 1.4–2.0 g protein per kg body weight daily (food + shakes).
TimingAny time you're short on protein — post-workout, breakfast, between meals.
Best forAnyone who struggles to eat enough protein from food.

Good to know: Whey isolate is lower in lactose and gentler on the stomach. Powder tops up real food — it doesn't replace balanced meals.

Ask about Whey Protein

Mass Gainer

Weight gain

A calorie-dense carb-plus-protein blend for those who struggle to eat enough to grow.

Key benefits

  • Easy extra calories for size & weight gain
  • Great for "hard gainers" & fast metabolisms
  • Carbs help fuel training and recovery
Dose1 serving = several hundred calories. Start with ½ serving and adjust to your needs.
TimingPost-workout or between meals as a top-up.
Best forUnderweight lifters / hard gainers who can't eat enough whole food.

Good to know: It's high in calories and sugar — not for a fat-loss goal. If you can eat enough real food, whole-food calories are usually the better choice.

Ask about Mass Gainer

L-Carnitine

Fat metabolism

An amino-acid-derived compound that helps shuttle fatty acids into cells to be burned for energy.

Key benefits

  • Popular in fat-loss & endurance phases
  • May help reduce markers of muscle damage / soreness
  • Most useful for vegetarians (lower dietary carnitine)
Dose500–2,000 mg per day (up to ~2 g is considered safe).
TimingBefore cardio / training, or with a carb-containing meal.
Best forCutting / endurance phases and vegetarian diets.

Good to know — honest take: In healthy, well-fed people the evidence for extra fat loss is modest and mixed. It's a support, not a magic burner — real results come from a calorie deficit plus training.

Ask about L-Carnitine

Multivitamin

Daily foundation

A daily blend of essential vitamins and minerals to cover the gaps a busy or restricted diet leaves behind.

Key benefits

  • Fills common micronutrient gaps
  • Supports energy metabolism & immunity
  • Useful "insurance" for hard-training bodies
Dose1 serving per day, as directed on the label.
TimingWith a meal (helps absorb fat-soluble vitamins).
Best forInconsistent diets, dieters in a deficit, busy schedules.

Good to know: A multivitamin supports — it doesn't replace — a balanced diet. More isn't better; stick to the label dose.

Ask about Multivitamins

Fish Oil (Omega-3)

Heart · joints · brain

Concentrated EPA & DHA omega-3 fats from fish — nutrients most everyday diets fall short on.

Key benefits

  • Supports heart, joint and brain health
  • May help manage inflammation & recovery
  • Supports healthy triglyceride levels
Dose~1–2 g combined EPA+DHA/day. (A "1000 mg fish oil" cap often has only ~300 mg EPA+DHA — check the label.)
TimingWith a meal that contains some fat.
Best forAnyone who rarely eats oily fish; joints & general health.

Good to know: Choose a purified, mercury-free product and keep it cool. Don't exceed ~2 g EPA+DHA/day from supplements without medical advice; check with your doctor if you're on blood thinners.
